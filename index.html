Past research.
This fall, I'm working with Prof. Ben Shields of the MIT Sloan School of Management and Broadway producer Ryan Bogner in developing strategic data-based marketing models for Broadway and Off-Broadway theatrical productions. In summer 2020, I assisted Prof. David Autor of the Economics Department as well as PhD student Juliette Fournier in using geographical distributions and transportation systems to study labor markets in France. I also worked with the MIT Quest for Intelligence to develop model visualizations surrounding the current COVID-19 pandemic.

dance has always been one of my greatest passions.

I started dancing at the age of five at China Dance and Arts in the Bay Area, studying ballet, classical and ethnic Chinese dance, jazz, contemporary, and lyrical styles and performing at annual showcases.

At MIT, I'm currently a senior choreographer for the MIT Asian Dance Team as well as the Director of Public Relations on the executive team. In addition, I'm a performing company member of pre-professional Boston dance group Dance Revelasian, which whom I competed at the Taoli Overseas Dance Competition regionals in Boston and went on to take home the Elite Platinum award and the highest score in our age division at the Taoli World East Coast Finals in Flushing, NY. I was featured on the Arts@MIT blog for my involvement in the MIT and Boston dance community.

I'm active in the music community at MIT and beyond.

I'm classically trained in both piano (studying with John McCarthy at the San Francisco Conservatory of Music) and cello. I have previously played in the Lynbrook High School Chamber and Symphony Orchestrass performing in Barcelona, Zaragoza, and Valencia in Spain (2018, Principal Cellist) as well as Carnegie Hall in New York City (2016, Assistant Principal Cellist). In addition, I also played in the MIT Symphony Orchestra in their 2018-2019 season.

I have always loved chamber music -- this past spring I played in the MIT Chamber Music Society as part of a piano trio, working on Dvorak's Piano Trio No. 3 in f minor. In addition, I'm one of the founding exec members and the ensemble director of MIT Cello++, MIT's one-and-only cello ensemble.

One of my favorite pasttimes is arranging music with my friends in my free time, translating pop, soundtrack, and video game music to classical instruments and techniques. Past arrangements include Bubblegum K. K. from Animal Crossing (5 cellos), Writing's on the Wall by Sam Smith (5 cellos), Love Shot by EXO (4 cellos), a mashup of Pachebel's Canon in D and Maroon 5's Memories (4 cellos) and To Die For by Sam Smith (5 cellos). Scores available upon request.

This year, I'm excited to help organize the Music Unites: Songs for Equity (MUSE) Project, a series of remote music-making endeavors aiming to bring attention to the contributions of BIPOC composers and musicians to the classical music world.
