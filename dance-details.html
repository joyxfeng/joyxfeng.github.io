Dance is one of my greatest passions.

I started dancing at the age of five at China Dance and Arts in the Bay Area, studying ballet, classical and ethnic Chinese dance, jazz, contemporary, and lyrical styles and performing at annual showcases. With my studio, I attended the 2011 and 2014 Shanghai International Children's Cultural and Arts Festival (SICCAF), emceeing for the 2011 show and dancing in the Opening and Closing Ceremonies for the 2014 show. In high school, I competed in the USA dance competition circuits, including the American Dance Awards, the Starpower International Talent Competition, and the Showbiz International Talent Competition. With Ultimate Gold ratings, "Storyteller" and "Moving Images" judges awards, and a 1st place finish in the Senior Open Solo division, I won the America's Dancer of the Year title in 2016. I also got the opportunity to compete in the Stars of the World International Talent Competition at their California regionals, taking home third place in the Senior Solo/Duo/Trio division as well as a scholarship.

At MIT, I'm currently a senior choreographer for the MIT Asian Dance Team as well as the Director of Public Relations on the executive team. In addition, I'm a performing company member of pre-professional Boston dance group Dance Revelasian, which whom I competed at the Taoli Overseas Dance Competition regionals in Boston and went on to take home the Elite Platinum award and the highest score in our age division at the Taoli World East Coast Finals in Flushing, NY. I was featured on the Arts@MIT blog for my involvement in the MIT and Boston dance community.
